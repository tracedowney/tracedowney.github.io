Projects

A working archive of things made across screens, materials, and spaces.

Each lane gets to keep its own mood, but the connective tissue is the same: clarity, patience, and a habit of documenting the work well enough for someone else to feel it too.

Quick note

Public to view does not mean free to reuse.

Digital

App development

Software work here is less about hype and more about usefulness: clean interfaces, clearer workflows, and experiments that turn rough ideas into working tools.

Product thinking

Interfaces with purpose

  • Landing pages and portfolio experiences
  • Internal tools and workflow helpers
  • Structure that makes projects easier to explain

Process

Systems that reduce friction

  • Breaking messy ideas into usable parts
  • Improving navigation, readability, and flow
  • Documenting decisions so projects stay maintainable

Outcome

Useful builds that hold together

  • Side projects with a clear point of view
  • Work that makes the next decision easier
  • Code and interfaces that explain themselves better

Visual

Photography

Still images give me a way to pay attention. This lane is for atmosphere, composition, observation, and the kinds of details that are easy to miss when everything moves too fast.

Mood

Quiet detail

  • Texture, light, shadow, and shape
  • Everyday scenes with a stronger point of view
  • Image sets that feel cohesive when seen together

Documentation

Projects worth recording well

  • Build progress and finished work
  • Before-and-after transformations
  • Visual references for future work

Selection

Images that can stand still for a while

  • Curated sets with a cohesive mood
  • Photographs that do more than merely document
  • Permission handled deliberately, never by assumption

Motion

Drone footage

Aerial footage changes the story. It can show scale, sequence, geography, and progress in a way that still images sometimes cannot.

Perspective

Context from above

  • Landscape and location context
  • Project documentation over time
  • Visual storytelling with clearer spatial logic

Use cases

Motion with a job to do

  • Renovation and property progress
  • Place-based editing and travel footage
  • Highlight reels that feel grounded, not generic

Story

Movement that adds information

  • Aerial overviews that clarify scale
  • Frames chosen for pattern, not just spectacle
  • Capture handled with safety and compliance in mind

Adornment

Jewelry

Jewelry lives at a scale where every decision shows. I like the mix of design, finish, material sensitivity, and the challenge of making something small feel deliberate.

Detail

Small pieces, high attention

  • Form, proportion, and visual balance
  • Material and hardware choices that affect the feel
  • Craft where finish quality really matters

Design

Wearable object-making

  • Pieces that hold up both visually and practically
  • A place for experimentation without losing refinement
  • Documentation that treats small objects with the same seriousness as larger builds

Finish

Objects meant to be looked at closely

  • Pieces that reward a closer read
  • Detail photography that respects the scale of the work
  • Groupings by material, silhouette, or technique

Form

Origami

Origami is one of the clearest examples of why I love precision. It is structure, patience, repetition, and delight condensed into paper.

Craft

Geometry made tangible

  • Clean folds and modular forms
  • Experiments in pattern and repetition
  • Practice that sharpens patience

Why it matters

Discipline in miniature

  • Small mistakes matter, so care matters
  • Instructions and execution both matter
  • Beautiful outcomes come from sequence and control

Payoff

Precision with some delight left in it

  • Models where sequence becomes visual rhythm
  • Paper choices that change the character of the fold
  • Finished pieces treated like design objects, not throwaway craft

Textile

Quilting

Quilting is where color, utility, rhythm, and long-form patience all meet. It is one of my favorite examples of order becoming warmth.

Pattern

Many small decisions, one finished whole

  • Layout, balance, and color relationships
  • Practical objects with a strong visual identity
  • Room for experimentation without losing structure

Process

Slow work on purpose

  • Planning that still leaves space for intuition
  • Technique as a form of problem-solving
  • Documentation that shows how pieces evolve

Warmth

Structure with a human pulse

  • Pattern work that still feels personal
  • Color decisions that carry the mood
  • Process notes that reveal the logic behind the finished piece

Restoration

Upholstery

I like projects that restore usefulness and character at the same time. Upholstery and reupholstery are part problem-solving, part craftsmanship, and part respect for what a piece can still become.

Rescue

Second life, not second-best

  • Reading how a piece was originally made
  • Choosing what to preserve and what to redo
  • Making functional objects feel cared for again

Judgment

Material choices that change the outcome

  • Fabric, finish, and durability decisions
  • Before-and-after stories that show the transformation
  • Attention to fit, shape, and detail

Result

Pieces that feel useful again

  • Transformations that preserve character
  • Construction details that influence comfort and longevity
  • Work that respects both history and everyday use

Making

Woodworking

Woodworking appeals to the part of me that likes honest materials, durable solutions, and learning through repetition until the work gets cleaner.

Function

Useful builds first

  • Pieces that solve real everyday needs
  • Builds shaped by utility, not just novelty
  • Design choices that respect the material

Skill

Learning through better repetition

  • Fit, finish, and process refinement
  • Understanding where precision matters most
  • Seeing improvement directly in the work

Outcome

Pieces that earn their place

  • Builds made to be used, not just admired
  • Lessons visible in the joinery, fit, and finish
  • Documentation that keeps the learning attached to the object

Progress

Renovation

Renovation work is full of sequencing, judgment, persistence, and problem-solving. It is one of the clearest places where planning and adaptability have to coexist.

Scope

Room-by-room progress stories

  • What changed, why it changed, and what it took
  • Before, during, and after documentation
  • Decisions that improved function and feel

Documentation

Proof of progress matters

  • Photos, video, and notes that make long projects legible
  • Lessons from sequencing and rework
  • Records useful for future planning

Arc

Long projects made legible

  • Milestones that show real movement
  • Before, during, and after seen in context
  • Notes that connect tiny decisions to the bigger change
