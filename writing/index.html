Notes on process, structure, and making.

This section is reserved for essays, build notes, and process writing that add context to the portfolio instead of just decorating it.

Build notes

Short pieces on what worked, what changed, and what the finished version taught me about the next one.

Process journals

Longer writing on renovation, quilting, woodworking, and other projects that only make sense when the middle is documented.

Cross-medium lessons

What software can learn from craft, what making can learn from systems, and why documentation improves both.

The thinking belongs in the portfolio too.

A lot of value disappears when a project is shown only as a finished artifact. Writing records the reasoning, clarifies the tradeoffs, and makes the work more useful to other people.

It also creates a record of what I am actually learning, which is often the most reusable part of the process.
