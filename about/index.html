About

A portfolio for someone who rarely stays in one lane.

I am drawn to work that rewards attention: interfaces that need structure, materials that need patience, spaces that need rethinking, and creative projects that get better the longer I stay with them.

The short version

This site is where the scattered pieces start to make sense together.

I am Trace Downey, and tracedowney.com is my attempt to bring the interesting parts of my work and interests into one place without sanding off the personality.

Some of that work is digital: app development, workflows, product thinking, and experiments that try to make life a little more usable. Some of it is visual: photography, drone footage, and the instinct to document progress well. Some of it is tactile: jewelry, origami, quilting, upholstery, woodworking, and renovation projects that teach through doing.

The overlap is what matters most to me. I like thoughtful systems, satisfying materials, and the challenge of making something coherent enough to share.

Core themes

What keeps showing up

  • Curiosity that starts with “what if this could work better?”
  • Strong preference for clarity over clutter
  • Respect for detail, whether the medium is code, fabric, wood, paper, or a camera timeline
  • Documentation that helps other people understand the work without needing extra translation

Why this matters

One site. Many expressions.

I want the site to feel broad enough for all the side things and focused enough to still feel unmistakably like me.

Approach

I like work with texture.

Not everything needs to look corporate or feel flattened. I want usefulness, warmth, and a little visual character.

Process

I care about systems.

Whether I am organizing a project, editing footage, or building a page, structure is part of the craft.

Sharing

I document as I go.

Some of the best value in a project comes from making the thinking legible, not just showing the finished result.
