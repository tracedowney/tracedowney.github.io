Rights & permissions

This site is for viewing, evaluating, and starting a conversation, not for unapproved reuse.

Unless a page says otherwise, the original writing, photography, footage, designs, project documentation, and code samples shown on tracedowney.com are presented for portfolio and informational purposes. Posting them publicly does not grant permission to reuse them.

Ownership

Unless otherwise noted, original content on this site is owned by Trace Downey or is displayed with appropriate permission from the relevant rights holder.

Generally permitted

  • Viewing the site in a normal browser
  • Sharing direct links to public pages
  • Referring to the work in a factual way with proper attribution and without reproducing the underlying content

Requires prior written permission

  • Reproducing, reposting, redistributing, or publishing site content in whole or in part
  • Commercial, editorial, promotional, or portfolio reuse of photographs, footage, writing, or project documentation
  • Editing, adapting, remixing, or creating derivative works from site content
  • Using site content for automated scraping, dataset creation, AI model training, or similar machine-learning uses

Third-party rights still apply

Some projects may involve collaborators, client constraints, brands, locations, software, or materials that carry their own rights or permissions issues. Permission from Trace Downey does not waive or replace any third-party rights that may also apply.

Request path

Need permission?

Send a written request to info@tracedowney.com with the specific material, intended use, audience, timing, and whether the request is commercial, editorial, educational, or personal.

Why this page exists

Clear notice is part of being above board.

The goal is to make ownership, expectations, and the permission path explicit. Publication is not a waiver of rights, and visibility is not the same thing as consent.
